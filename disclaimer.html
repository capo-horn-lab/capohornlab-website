2. Backtesting Limitations

Backtesting involves the simulation of a trading strategy using historical data to assess how it would have performed in the past. It is important to understand the inherent limitations of backtesting:

  • Hypothetical Nature: All backtesting results are hypothetical and do not represent actual trading. They are generated by applying a strategy to historical market data and may not account for all real-world factors that affect live trading.
  • Slippage and Execution: Our models include realistic slippage estimates, but actual slippage during live trading may differ significantly due to market conditions, order book depth, latency, and execution venue.
  • Look-Ahead Bias: We design our backtesting pipeline to prevent look-ahead bias, but no methodology can guarantee that the future will resemble the past.
  • Overfitting Risk: Strategies that perform well in backtesting may be overfitted to historical data and fail to generalise to unseen market conditions.
  • Market Regime Changes: Market microstructure, volatility regimes, and correlation structures change over time. A strategy that was profitable in historical backtests may perform poorly or incur losses in live trading.

You should never allocate capital to any strategy based solely on backtesting results. All backtesting should be considered one input among many in your trading decisions.
