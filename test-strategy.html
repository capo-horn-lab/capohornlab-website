Strategy Identity

Tell us about your strategy — its name, logic, and what it trades.

💡
Precision matters. A strategy described as "buy when it goes up" cannot be tested. A strategy described as "enter long when the 20-period SMA crosses above the 50-period SMA on the 5-minute chart" can. The more specific you are, the more accurate your results will be.
Choose a descriptive name for your strategy. This is how it will appear in your dashboard.

Time Settings

Step 2 of 7

Entry Rules

Describe the conditions that trigger a position entry.

💡
Detail is better than brevity. If your strategy uses multiple indicators, list them all. If there is a sequence (e.g. "first check A, then wait for B"), describe it. Ambiguous rules produce ambiguous results.
What specific market condition must be met before you enter? Include indicator values, price levels, or pattern recognition logic. Be precise — "RSI below 30 and price closes above SMA(20)" is clearer than "oversold condition."
Which indicators does your strategy use, and what are their specific parameters?
Any additional filters that must be true before entry?
Maximum number of entries per trading day.
Step 3 of 7

Exit Rules

Define how and when positions are closed.

💡
Define risk before reward. A stop loss is required for every strategy. Take profit, trailing stops, and break-even logic are optional but recommended — they define how the strategy manages open positions.
How does your strategy define the maximum acceptable loss per trade?
At what profit level does the strategy exit?
Does the strategy use a trailing stop? If so, describe the trail distance and activation condition.
Does the strategy move the stop to break-even after a certain profit level?
Does the strategy exit based on time rather than price?
Step 4 of 7

Execution Parameters

Set contract sizes, costs, and execution details.

💡
Honest slippage matters. The biggest gap between backtest and live trading is execution. Using 0 slippage and $0 commissions will produce beautiful equity curves that do not survive real markets. Be realistic.
How many contracts does your strategy trade per entry?
How is position size determined?
Commission cost per contract per side. Default reflects standard retail futures brokerage.
Expected slippage in ticks. Default: 1 tick (ES), 2 ticks (NQ). Adjust if you have empirical data on your fill quality.
What order type does your strategy use to enter and exit?
