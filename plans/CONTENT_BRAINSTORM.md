## 1. Brand Voice & Tone of Voice

### 1.1 The Brand Archetype: The Scientist-Explorer

Capo Horn Lab sits at the intersection of two archetypes:

| Archetype | Manifestation |
|-----------|---------------|
| **The Scientist** | Methodical, falsifiable, reproducible. Every claim is backed by data. We publish negative results because truth is more valuable than a good story. |
| **The Explorer** | We navigate uncharted waters — the market's microstructure, the patterns others dismiss as noise. Cape Horn is the real place that inspired the name: the southernmost point of the Americas, where the Atlantic and Pacific collide in treacherous, data-rich waters. |

**Brand voice = cold precision + quiet conviction.**

### 1.2 Voice Spectrum

| Axis | Always | Never |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Tone** | Confident but humble. Authoritative but not arrogant. Warm but not salesy. | Hype, urgency, FOMO, "get rich," "secret," "proven system" |
| **Vocabulary** | "Hypothesis," "falsifiable," "reproducible," "metric," "edge," "distribution," "statistical significance" | "Guaranteed," "easy money," "100% win rate," "insider," "guru," "secret formula" |
| **Sentence structure** | Declarative, concise. Short paragraphs. Bullet points where precise. | Run-on marketing copy. Wall-of-text testimonials. |
| **Pronouns** | "We" (lab identity), "You" (client in CTAs only), "Our research shows…" | Overuse of "I" — the lab speaks, not an individual. |
| **Data presentation** | Tables, charts, quantitative summaries. "Sharpe: 0.12 IS / -0.08 OOS" | Vague percentages. Cherry-picked best-case metrics. |

### 1.3 The Voice in Three Sentences

1. **We test ideas against real data and publish what we find — including when it hurts.**
2. **Every page on this site is an argument for rigour over hype.**
3. **We don't sell strategies. We sell truth, reproducibility, and the quiet satisfaction of knowing what actually works.**

### 1.4 Written Samples

**Too salesy (AVOID):**
> "Discover the secret strategy that top traders don't want you to know! Join thousands of successful investors who've transformed their trading with our proven system. Start making money today!"

**Too academic (also avoid — no soul):**
> "The present study investigates the efficacy of impulse-retracement-breakout patterns as a predictive signal for E-mini S&P 500 futures contracts during the period 2020-2024. Results indicate no statistically significant outperformance net of transaction costs."

**Capo Horn Lab voice (target):**
> "We tested whether impulse-retracement patterns on ES have predictive power. The patterns exist — confirmed. But after commissions and slippage, the edge vanishes. Read the full breakdown, with charts, metrics, and the code we used."

### 1.5 Key Phrases to Use Across the Site

| Context | Phrase |
|---------|--------|
| Mission | "We backtest strategies on real historical data. No strategy selling. No signals. No subscriptions." |
| Value prop | "Bring us your strategy. We'll tell you if it works — and why." |
| Research | "Published quantitative research. Results you can reproduce." |
| Negative result | "We spent computing time to prove this didn't work. Here's why that's useful." |
| Method | "Tick-level data. Institutional-grade rigour. Honest conclusions." |
| CTA | "Have a strategy? Put it to the test." |

### 2.3 METHOD

**Objective:** Demonstrate rigour without overwhelming. Convince the visitor that this is serious, institutional-grade backtesting.

**Headline**
```
How We Backtest
Rigour. Reproducibility. Realism.
```

**Pipeline Visual (text-based or diagram)**
```
Idea → Formalise Rules → Code → Run on Tick Data →
Statistical Analysis → Interpretation → Publication
```

**Rigour Standards (accordion or cards)**

| Standard | What It Means |
|----------|---------------|
| **IS/OOS Split** | 70% in-sample for development, 30% out-of-sample for validation. Never test on the same data you trained on. |
| **Monte Carlo Simulation** | 1,000+ randomised trade sequences to estimate the range of possible outcomes. |
| **Parameter Stability** | We vary parameters across a grid and measure whether performance is robust or a product of data-mining. |
| **Transaction Costs** | Realistic slippage and commissions for each instrument. No "theoretical" results. |
| **Multiple Timeframes** | A strategy that only works on 1m but fails on 5m or 1h is a red flag. |

**Data Section**
```
| Parameter | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| Tick data sources | Databento MDP3 |
| Instruments | ES, NQ, CL, 6E (+ others on request) |
| Historical depth | 2020–present (growing) |
| Resolution | Tick → 1m / 5m / 15m / 1h / Daily |
| Slippage model | 1 tick ES, 2 ticks NQ (configurable) |
| Commissions | $2.50 per side per contract (configurable) |
```

**FAQ Accordion (copy)**

| Question | Answer |
|----------|--------|
| "Do you sell strategies?" | No. We test yours. We never sell strategies, indicators, or signals. |
| "How is this different from TradingView backtesting?" | We use tick-level data, realistic slippage, Monte Carlo simulation, and IS/OOS validation. TradingView bar-replay is not a substitute. |
| "How long does it take?" | Typically 3–10 business days depending on complexity. |
| "Do you test crypto?" | Not in MVP. Futures only (ES, NQ, CL, 6E). |
| "What if my strategy doesn't work?" | That's the most valuable outcome. A clear "this doesn't work" saves you time and capital. |
| "Can I see an example?" | Yes — all our published research is available on the Research page. |

| Rule | Example | Anti-example |
|------|---------|--------------|
| Action-oriented, not benefit-oriented | "Submit Your Strategy" | "Start Making Profits" |

- **Primary button:** Accent blue (`#3B82F6`) on dark navy (`#0F172A`) background

- **No glow effects, no gradients, no emoji in buttons**

## 5. Blog & Research Content Strategy

### 5.1 Content Pillars

|--------|-------------|----------------|
| **Published Research** | Full quantitative studies with charts and methodology. The core output. | ES 1m price-only, Structure patterns, Volume profile |
| **Methodology Deep-Dives** | How we do what we do. Process transparency. | IS/OOS explained, Monte Carlo for traders, Slippage modelling |

### 5.2 Blog Post Formats

**1. Full Research Report** (core format)
- Structure: Hypothesis → Data → Methodology → Results → Charts → Interpretation → Limitations
- Length: 800–2000 words
- Required: All MVP charts (7 minimum)
- PDF version for download

**2. Method / Process Article** (1 per quarter)
- Structure: Problem → Approach → Example → Why it matters
- Length: 500–1200 words
- Example: "Why We Use Out-of-Sample Testing"

**3. Data Snapshot** (lightweight, 1–2 per quarter)
- Structure: Question → One chart → 2-paragraph analysis
- Length: 200–400 words
- Example: "What Does ES Tick Distribution Look Like?"

**4. Aggregate / Review** (1 per half-year)
- Structure: What we tested → What we found → What's next
- Length: 600–1000 words

## 7. Copywriting Guidelines & Anti-Patterns

### 7.1 The "No" List

These words and phrases are **banned** across the entire site:

```
❌ "proven" (nothing is proven in markets)
❌ "guaranteed" 
❌ "secret" / "hidden" / "revealed"
❌ "guru" / "expert" / "master"
❌ "easy" / "simple" (trading is neither)
❌ "instant" / "immediate"
❌ "passive income" / "financial freedom"
❌ "millionaire" / "wealth"
❌ "win rate" without context (always pair with profit factor and Sharpe)
❌ "backtested" without specifying slippage and commission assumptions
```

### 7.2 The "Always" List

